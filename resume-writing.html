Resume Writing Services

Need help with your resume? With the right guidance, your resume can stand out from the rest, getting the attention that it deserves.

Resume Writing

A resume is often the most important document of our professional life, laying down how and why we are wherever we are, and what we can do the best. All with an underlying essence of our personality. Looking for a job can be quite an unnerving experience.

Getting that first interview call really matters. A lot depends on making the right first impression. A good resume can make all the difference.

You absolutely know that you need a great resume which is going to stand out, but you don't have the required skills or the time to write one yourself. A good resume is crucial for getting your foot in the door for your perfect dream job.

An average recruiter spends less than 10 minutes reading each resume. If your resume doesn’t stand out, you’re not likely to receive a call back.

Professional resume writing services help you create a resume that will make employers sit up and take notice. Our team of experts knows how to highlight your skills and experience in the best possible light, so you can get the job you always wanted.

Why do I need Resume Writing Services?

An average interviewer goes through many resumes for a single job opening. All the more important to make your resume stand out. Professional resume writing services get you a skill-based resume that showcases all your strengths in the best possible manner.

Including the right keywords emphasizing your career goals and achievements is a good way to grab attention and hold it.

More and better employment opportunities are among the top reasons to migrate overseas. While looking for a job abroad is quite simple, getting hired by an overseas employer is another matter altogether. A perfect resume is required to secure a suitable job.

A well-written resume, with the industry-related keywords included at all the right places, can indeed make all the difference.

With Kansas Overseas Resume Writing Services, you can make a mark where it counts the most.

What can I get from Resume Writing Services?

  • Complete analysis
  • Holistic approach
  • Bring out your strengths
  • Minimize your weaknesses, if any
  • Collaborative approach
  • Current trends
  • Cover Letter
  • ATS-optimized

Put simply, a resume is a medium for pitching yourself to your future employer. A resume should tell a story. Everyone has their own story to tell. The more compelling your narrative is, the more the chances of your resume finding its way to the higher levels, the decision-makers of the organization or company.

Adopting a holistic approach is important, giving the reader of your resume a clear and complete analysis of your educational background and professional journey. While you get your study and career path clearly laid down, you must always add value to the reader. Talk facts, but get to the point.

Keeping it simple, short, and to the point is more important than ever in the highly-competitive world that we find ourselves in. A lengthy narrative might appear to be dragging and off-course.

A concise facts-only story, on the other hand, has all the potential for really piquing the reader’s curiosity. A curiosity that would naturally translate into interest.

An understanding of the current industry trends is also important to understand what precisely the company might be looking for in a specific position. Find out what are the most sought-after skills and strengths in your industry at the moment.

Another key factor, often underestimated by applicants, is the Cover Letter. Don’t make the common mistake of going with the same Cover Letter for every position that you apply to.

A unique Cover Letter should ideally be written for each job opening that you apply to. The impression to be conveyed here is that you have taken the time and made the effort to apply.

Dashing off the same Cover Letter to all - only changing the addressee and company details - may be quicker, but it might also work against you.

Over 70% of resumes that are submitted never reach human eyes. A majority get filtered through the Application Tracking System (ATS), a software used by companies for shortlisting candidates.

Scanning resumes and other job-related documents against a set of pre-determined keywords - such as job titles, technical skills, and educational background - the ATS determines whether or not an applicant is qualified for a specific job opening.

Passing the automated ATS scan and progressing onto the interview stage takes a bit of work. To be able to pass the ATS scan, your resume must have all the right words in the correct formatting. Getting an ATS-friendly resume is the perfect way to land your dream job.

What is an ATS-friendly resume?

  • No special fonts
  • No special characters
  • Font size 10-12 points
  • Strategically-placed keywords
  • 1-inch border

Here, ATS stands for the Application Tracking System, software used by companies and recruiters to sift through the profiles on their terms. A resume must be ATS-friendly to make it past the initial screening stage.

An ATS-friendly resume is one that is formatted using an ATS-compliant resume template, contains all the industry-specific keywords, and is worth the time of the human recruiter.

Resumes that get through the ATS are the ones that reach the hiring manager. The rest generally don’t make it beyond the initial screening process, getting held-up and stalled by the ATS.

As per an estimate, reportedly around 75% of applicants are disqualified by the ATS software itself. Never to reach a hiring manager that might actually get down to reading the resume.

An ATS-friendly resume is specifically designed for getting a green signal from the Application Tracking System put in place by a particular company.

The cornerstone of an ATS-compliant resume is the ease and readability quotient. A resume that is difficult to read and understand, using complex and ambiguous words, might not get past the ATS.

Similarly, removing images and tables and including keywords from the given job description make your resume more ATS-friendly.

Always create your resume with ATS software in mind, thereby ensuring that your job application stands a much better chance to reach a hiring manager.

How to make an ATS-friendly resume?

There are many ways to make sure that your resume gets past the initial screening ATS software. Any changes made in your resume with ATS in mind can definitely increase the chances of your resume being shortlisted and forwarded to the right channel.

Five ways to make your resume more ATS-friendly -

  • Include the job-related keywords
  • Proper labeling
  • Chronological format
  • ATS-friendly template
  • Save in the right format

ATS software scans your resume for various industry-specific and job-related keywords relevant to the position applied for. While some ATS software might use Artificial Intelligence (AI) to determine your eligibility, older software might simply scan your resume for certain keywords.

Include the words from the job listing in your resume. A match between the job listing keywords and the resume or cover letter resume keywords increase the likelihood of your profile getting into the interview pool.

The specific skills mentioned in the job description should be included in the skills or core competencies section on your resume, thereby signaling the ATS that you are qualified for the job in consideration.

The importance of proper labeling can hardly be overstated. Avoid playing around with headings to come up with something that might confuse the ATS, for example, “why I’m great?”, “jobs I’ve done”. Keep it simple and basic. Put the commonly-used headings, such as,

The specific skills mentioned in the job description should be included in the skills or core competencies section on your "skills”, “work experience”, “education” etc.

Provided there are no long gaps in the employment history, the chronological resume is the most suitable format for most applicants. Beginning from the current or most recent employment, proceed on to the previous work experience in chronological order, ending with your first job.

The chronological approach is the best way for emphasizing and organizing your work experience.

Select an ATS-friendly resume template. Keep in mind that resumes that have unique fonts or too many graphic elements might be difficult for ATS software to scan.

Fonts to be used in a resume must be those commonly-used and easy to read for the ATS software. It is recommended to stick to the basic fonts, such as Arial, Times New Roman etc.

If there is no specific requirement as to the kind of file format to save your resume in, you can save your resume either as a pdf or in .docx format. Older ATS software might face issues with understanding resume pdf files.

What are the top mistakes to avoid when writing resumes?

The common mistakes to avoid when writing resumes are -

  • Generalization: You can’t use the same resume to apply for different job positions.
  • Grammatical errors: Grammar lapses generally tend to stand out and are duly noted by the companies.
  • Spelling mistakes: Using wrong spellings in your resume can cost you your dream job.
  • Unprofessional email id: Your email id must be the one to be used for professional purposes. Don’t provide an email address that might appear unprofessional.
  • Forced keywords: Avoid using keywords that might bring down your credibility. Don’t unnecessarily stuff keywords when there is no place for them given the context.
  • Being vague: Be specific, precise and to the point. Don't use vague words like many, few, some, several etc.
  • Fancy fonts: The font used by you must be readable across different online platforms. Stick to the common fonts. Don’t use fonts that might not be read by some devices or software.
  • Giving too much information: Extra information can overwhelm and distract an employer. Avoid sharing more information than what might be needed, given the circumstances.
  • Divulging personal details: Keep in mind that you don’t have to give personal information to recruiters.
  • Irrelevant social media handles: With the job description in mind, select the social media handles that you must be mentioning

What jobs come under specialty occupations for the H-1B?

  • Theoretical or technical expertise needed
  • Must be a graduate-level job

H-1B specialty occupations might include - the arts, business specialties, accounting, physical sciences, education, engineering, mathematics, social sciences, theology, law, medicine and health, and architecture. H-1B visas are available for different types of specialty occupations that need expertise - theoretical or technical - in specialized fields.

Simply put, any job available in the American labor market might come under the H-1B requirement, provided it requires a minimum of a Bachelor’s degree. To be eligible for the H-1B visa, the nature of the specific duties must be so specialized and complex that they could only be done by someone with a bachelor’s or higher degree.

For the H-1B, specialty occupations might be, among others -

  • Engineers
  • Architects
  • Nurses
  • System analysts
  • IT/computer professionals
  • Accountants
  • Financial analysts
  • Healthcare workers
  • Management consultants
  • Scientists
  • Dentists
  • Physicians
  • Surgeons
  • Dentists
  • Psychologists
  • Foreign law advisors
  • Journalists and editors
  • Market research analysts

The above-mentioned are only some of the US jobs that come under specialty occupations. Any position, as long as it meets the qualifications requiring specialization, can come under the classification.

Can I get US Green Card through H-1B?

If you are in the US on an H-1B visa and wish to transition onto a US Green Card - officially referred to as the Permanent Resident Card - you might be able to perpetually extend your H-1B status in the US. For this, you will have to file for an EB-1, EB-2, or EB-3 (employment-based) visa before the end of your fifth year in the US on H-1B status.

You may be eligible to apply for a US Green Card (permanent residency) while holding an H-1B visa. However, you must apply for the Green Card by the end of the second year of your H-1B extension.

Do note that only the time actually spent by the worker in the US with H-1B status will be counted towards the six years. Any time spent by the individual outside the US will not be counted, even if exit and subsequent entry into the United States has been made while on H-1B status.

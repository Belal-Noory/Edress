Canada Business Immigration

Canada is one of the easiest countries in the world to run your own business. Experienced businessmen around the world are moving to Canada because it offers European-style social benefits with better quality of life. There are various benefits of moving to Canada as a business immigrant like coverage under the national health care program, access to a first-class education, and a national pension system.

By having your business in Canada, you can take advantage of having low corporate tax rates, Canadian trade pacts, cultural diversity, and also get Canadian permanent residency.

Main Categories of Business Visas

Now to attract experienced businessmen to buy or own a business in the country, Canada offers them different immigration Business visas. The major categories under Canada business immigration are:

Self-Employed Visa

PNP Entrepreneur Visa

Business Visitor Visa

Start-up Visa

Self-Employed Visa

The Self-Employed program allows the self-employed person to move to Canada as a permanent resident. To qualify under this program, you must:

Have relevant work experience in cultural activities or athletics; and Contribute significantly to the country's cultural and athletic life.

After you have met the definition of a Self-Employed person, you have to score 35 out of 100 points based on the following criteria:

Education (Maximum 25 points)

Experience (Maximum 35 points)

Age (Maximum 10 points)

Language Skills - English or French (Maximum 24 points)

Adaptability (Maximum 6 points)

One of the main features of Canada's Self-Employed program is that there is no minimum net-worth requirement. Also, applicants are required to have at least two years of work experience in their respective fields of work to qualify for this program.

Start-up Visa

The Start-up Visa program is for innovative entrepreneurs to establish their own business in Canada. It is the best option if you are looking to immigrate to Canada as an investor.

Applicants are eligible for a temporary work permit that allows them to establish their business in the country while waiting for their permanent residency.

To be eligible for a Canada Start-up visa, you must meet the following requirements:

A Qualifying business;

Language skills with minimum Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) level 5;

Letter of Support from a Designated Entity;

Provide proof of sufficient funds;

Business Visitor Visa

With a Business Visitor Visa, you can come to Canada for any international business activity or you are visiting the country temporarily for looking to grow your business.

If you are moving to Canada on a Business Visitor visa, you won't need a work permit. The maximum validity of this visa is 6 months.

To show you are a business visitor, you have to show:

You are not planning to stay for more than 6 months.

You are not entering the Canadian labor market.

Your main source of income and business is outside of Canada.

Supporting documentation for your application.

Meet Canada's basic entry requirements (Proof of funds, Valid travel documents, etc.).
